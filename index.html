Four-Stage Simulation Pipeline

A comprehensive computational framework for predicting safe and effective BBB opening

01

CT Scan Conversion

Converting CT scans to acoustic property maps using the Aubry method, transforming Hounsfield Units into detailed acoustic property distributions.

02

Acoustic Wave Propagation

Simulating acoustic wave propagation through brain tissue using a k-space pseudospectral solver, accurately modeling ultrasound beam focusing.

03

Microbubble Dynamics

Modeling microbubble behavior using the Marmottant equation to predict cavitation effects and bubble radius excursions during ultrasound exposure.

04

Thermal Effects Estimation

Estimating temperature rise using the bioheat equation to ensure thermal safety and prevent tissue damage during treatment.

The BBB Opening Process

Step-by-step visualization of focused ultrasound-mediated barrier disruption

Before FUS

1. Initial State

Tight junctions between endothelial cells create an impermeable barrier, blocking drug molecules from entering the brain tissue.

FUS Application

2. Ultrasound Application

Focused ultrasound waves target the barrier, causing microbubbles to oscillate and expand, creating mechanical stress on the endothelial cells.

Barrier Opening

3. Temporary Opening

Tight junctions temporarily separate, creating transient gaps that allow therapeutic agents to cross into the brain parenchyma.

Recovery

4. Barrier Recovery

After drug delivery, the barrier naturally reseals, restoring its protective function while therapeutic agents remain in the brain tissue.
